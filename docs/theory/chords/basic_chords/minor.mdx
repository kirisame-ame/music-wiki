# Minor Chords

Minor chords create a darker, more melancholic sound compared to major chords. They are built using the 1st, flat 3rd, and 5th degrees of the major scale.

## Structure

A minor chord consists of:

- **Root** - The fundamental note that gives the chord its name
- **Minor Third** - 3 semitones above the root
- **Perfect Fifth** - 7 semitones above the root

## Examples:

- C minor: **C - Eb - G**
- D minor: **D - F - A**
- E minor: **E - G - B**
- F minor: **F - Ab - C**

## Sound Characteristics

- **Mood**: Sad, dark, introspective
- **Symbol**: Cm, Cmin, or C-

Minor chords are essential for creating emotional depth in music and are frequently used to convey feelings of sadness, mystery, or tension.
