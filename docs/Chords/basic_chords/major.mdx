# Major Chords

Major chords create a bright, happy sound. They are built using the 1st, 3rd, and 5th degrees of the major scale.

## Structure

A major chord consists of:

- **Root** - The fundamental note that gives the chord its name
- **Major Third** - 4 semitones above the root
- **Perfect Fifth** - 7 semitones above the root

## Examples:

<SheetDisplay mxl="/mxl/chords/major_chords.mxl" />
<MidiPlayer src="/midi/chords/major_chords.mid" />

- C major: **C - E - G**
- D major: **D - F# - A**
- E major: **E - G# - B**
- F major: **F - A - C**

## Sound Characteristics

- **Mood**: Bright, happy, stable
- **Symbol**: C, Cmaj, or CM

Major chords form the basis for most chord progressions and are essential for understanding harmony in music.
